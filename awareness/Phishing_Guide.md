# Phishing Awareness Guide

## Purpose

The purpose of this guide is to help employees identify, prevent, and report phishing attacks that may compromise company systems or sensitive information.

---

# What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyberattack in which attackers pretend to be trusted individuals or organizations to steal sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, financial details, or personal information.

---

# Common Types of Phishing

## Email Phishing

Fraudulent emails that appear to come from trusted organizations.

Example:

- Fake bank email
- Fake Microsoft login
- Fake company notification

---

## Spear Phishing

A targeted phishing attack aimed at a specific individual or organization.

---

## Smishing

Phishing through SMS or text messages.

---

## Vishing

Phishing through phone calls pretending to be technical support or banks.

---

# Warning Signs

Look for these warning signs:

- Unknown sender
- Poor grammar and spelling
- Urgent requests
- Threatening language
- Suspicious links
- Unexpected attachments
- Requests for passwords
- Fake company logos

---

# How to Identify Phishing Emails

Before clicking anything:

- Check the sender's email address.
- Hover over links to verify the destination.
- Look for spelling mistakes.
- Verify unexpected requests.
- Contact the sender through an official communication channel if unsure.

---

# What to Do if You Receive a Phishing Email

1. Do not click any links.
2. Do not open attachments.
3. Do not reply to the email.
4. Report the email to the IT department.
5. Delete the email after reporting.

---

# If You Clicked a Phishing Link

Immediately:

- Disconnect from the network if instructed by IT.
- Change your password.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
- Inform the IT department immediately.
- Monitor your account for suspicious activity.

---

# Prevention Tips

- Use strong passwords.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication.
- Keep software updated.
- Use antivirus software.
- Avoid downloading files from unknown sources.
- Verify unexpected requests.

---

# Reporting Procedure

When reporting a phishing attempt, include:

- Date and time
- Sender's email address
- Subject line
- Screenshots (if available)
- Description of the incident

---

# Best Practices

- Think before you click.
- Verify before you trust.
- Report suspicious emails immediately.
- Never share passwords by email.
- Stay informed about new phishing techniques.

---

# Review

This guide should be reviewed every year and updated whenever new phishing techniques become common.
