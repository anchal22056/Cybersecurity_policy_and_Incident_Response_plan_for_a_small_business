# Risk Matrix

## Purpose

A Risk Matrix is used to determine the overall risk level by comparing the likelihood of a threat with its potential impact on the organization.

---

# Risk Rating Scale

## Likelihood

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Low | Rare occurrence |
| Medium | Occasional occurrence |
| High | Frequent occurrence |

---

## Impact

| Rating | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Low | Minimal business impact |
| Medium | Moderate business impact |
| High | Severe business impact |

---

# Risk Matrix

| Likelihood | Low Impact | Medium Impact | High Impact |
|------------|------------|---------------|-------------|
| High | Medium | High | Critical |
| Medium | Low | Medium | High |
| Low | Low | Low | Medium |

---

# Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Overall Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|--------|--------------|------------|
| Malware | High | High | Critical | Antivirus and Endpoint Protection |
| Phishing | High | High | Critical | Security Awareness Training |
| Ransomware | Medium | High | High | Regular Backups |
| Insider Threat | Low | High | Medium | Access Control |
| Power Failure | Medium | Medium | Medium | UPS and Backup Power |
| Hardware Failure | Medium | Medium | Medium | Preventive Maintenance |
| Data Breach | Medium | High | High | Encryption and MFA |
| Weak Passwords | High | Medium | High | Strong Password Policy |

---

# Risk Treatment

For each identified risk, the organization may choose one of the following strategies:

- Avoid the risk.
- Reduce the risk.
- Transfer the risk.
- Accept the risk.

---

# Review

The Risk Matrix should be updated annually or whenever significant changes occur in the organization's systems or business processes.
