# Risk Assessment

## Purpose

The purpose of this Risk Assessment is to identify, evaluate, and manage cybersecurity risks that could affect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of the organization's information systems and data.

---

# Scope

This assessment applies to:

- Computers and laptops
- Servers
- Network devices
- Cloud services
- Email systems
- Business applications
- Employee devices
- Customer information

---

# Risk Assessment Process

The organization follows these steps:

1. Identify assets.
2. Identify threats.
3. Identify vulnerabilities.
4. Assess likelihood.
5. Assess business impact.
6. Calculate risk level.
7. Implement security controls.
8. Review risks regularly.

---

# Assets

- Desktop Computers
- Laptops
- File Server
- Wi-Fi Router
- Internet Connection
- Customer Database
- Financial Records
- Employee Information
- Email Server
- Cloud Storage

---

# Threats

- Malware
- Ransomware
- Phishing
- Data Breach
- Insider Threat
- Hardware Failure
- Power Outage
- Unauthorized Access
- Social Engineering
- Denial-of-Service (DoS)

---

# Vulnerabilities

- Weak passwords
- Outdated software
- Missing security updates
- Lack of employee awareness
- Poor backup practices
- Misconfigured firewall
- Unencrypted sensitive data
- Insecure Wi-Fi network

---

# Existing Security Controls

- Antivirus software
- Firewall
- Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
- Strong password policy
- Regular backups
- Security awareness training
- Access control policy
- Email filtering

---

# Risk Evaluation

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Risk Level |
|------|------------|--------|------------|
| Malware | High | High | Critical |
| Phishing | High | High | Critical |
| Data Breach | Medium | High | High |
| Hardware Failure | Medium | Medium | Medium |
| Insider Threat | Low | High | Medium |

---

# Risk Mitigation

- Update software regularly.
- Enable Multi-Factor Authentication.
- Perform regular backups.
- Conduct employee security training.
- Use endpoint protection software.
- Monitor network traffic.
- Encrypt sensitive information.
- Restrict user permissions.

---

# Review Schedule

Risk assessments should be performed:

- Every 12 months
- After major security incidents
- After significant infrastructure changes
