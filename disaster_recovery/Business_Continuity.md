# Business Continuity Plan (BCP)

## Purpose

The purpose of the Business Continuity Plan (BCP) is to ensure that essential business operations continue during and after a disaster, cyberattack, or other unexpected disruption.

---

# Scope

This plan applies to:

- Employees
- Information systems
- Business operations
- Customers
- Vendors
- Critical business services

---

# Business Objectives

The organization aims to:

- Maintain critical business operations.
- Reduce operational downtime.
- Protect customer information.
- Maintain communication.
- Restore services quickly.

---

# Critical Business Functions

| Function | Priority |
|----------|----------|
| Customer Support | High |

| Internet Connectivity | High |
| Financial Operations | High |
| Payroll | Medium |
| Printing Services | Low |

---

# Business Impact Analysis (BIA)

| Business Function | Maximum Downtime | Recovery Priority |
|------------------|------------------|-------------------|
| Customer Database | 4 Hours | Critical |
| Email System | 2 Hours | High |
| Financial System | 4 Hours | High |
| Website | 6 Hours | Medium |
| Office Printer | 24 Hours | Low |

---

# Continuity Strategies

- Maintain regular data backups.
- Use cloud-based storage.
- Enable remote work.
- Maintain spare hardware.
- Keep updated antivirus software.
- Use backup internet connectivity.

---

# Emergency Procedures

During a disruption:

1. Notify management.
2. Activate the Business Continuity Team.
3. Assess the situation.
4. Continue critical operations.
5. Restore affected systems.
6. Resume normal business operations.

---

# Employee Responsibilities

Employees must:

- Follow company policies.
- Report incidents immediately.
- Protect company information.
- Follow emergency instructions.
- Participate in continuity training.

---

External Communication:

# Recovery Resources

- Backup servers
- Cloud services
- UPS
- Emergency contact list
- Disaster recovery documentation

---

# Plan Testing

Business Continuity Plan testing should be performed:

- Every 6 months
- After major changes
- After significant incidents

---

# Plan Review

The Business Continuity Plan should be reviewed annually and updated whenever business processes or technology change.
