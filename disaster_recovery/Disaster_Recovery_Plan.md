# Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP)

## Purpose

The purpose of this Disaster Recovery Plan (DRP) is to provide a structured approach for restoring critical business systems, applications, and data after a disaster or major cybersecurity incident. The goal is to minimize downtime, reduce data loss, and ensure business continuity.

---

# Scope

This plan applies to:

- Company servers
- Desktop computers
- Laptops
- Network devices
- Cloud services
- Email systems
- Customer databases
- Financial systems

---

# Objectives

The objectives of this Disaster Recovery Plan are to:

- Restore critical systems quickly.
- Minimize business interruption.
- Protect important business data.
- Ensure employee and customer safety.
- Resume normal business operations.

---

## Recovery Time Objective (RTO)

Maximum acceptable downtime:

**4 Hours**

---

## Recovery Point Objective (RPO)

Maximum acceptable data loss:

**24 Hours**

---

# Disaster Recovery Process

## Step 1: Incident Detection

- Detect disaster.
- Inform management.
- Activate Disaster Recovery Team.

---

## Step 2: Damage Assessment

- Identify affected systems.
- Estimate business impact.
- Prioritize critical services.

---

## Step 3: Containment

- Disconnect affected systems.
- Protect unaffected systems.
- Preserve important evidence if required.

---

## Step 4: Recovery

- Restore backups.
- Repair damaged hardware.
- Recover applications.
- Restore network connectivity.

---

## Step 5: Validation

- Test recovered systems.
- Verify data integrity.
- Confirm business services are operational.

---

## Step 6: Return to Normal Operations

- Resume normal business.
- Continue monitoring.
- Close recovery activities.
